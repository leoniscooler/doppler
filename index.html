1. When the source moves, each new wave is emitted from a slightly different place.

2. Waves crowd together in front of the source and spread out behind it — this is the Doppler effect.

3. At Mach 1 (source speed = wave speed), wavefronts pile up at the source — the front wavelength approaches zero.

4. Above Mach 1, a V-shaped shockwave cone continuously trails behind the source, producing a sonic boom wherever it crosses an observer.
